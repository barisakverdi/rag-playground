# North England Regional Operations Report
**Period:** 1–31 March 2024  
**Prepared by:** Sarah Mitchell, Regional Operations Manager – North  
**Distribution:** Head of Operations, Supply Chain Lead, HR Business Partner

---

## Executive Summary

March has been a challenging month across the North England region. A combination of supplier delays, unresolved equipment faults, and ongoing staffing gaps has placed pressure on both the Leeds Central and Manchester Piccadilly branches. Customer satisfaction scores have dipped for the second consecutive month, and we are at risk of missing our Q1 service targets if corrective action is not taken before the end of April.

## Leeds Central Branch

Leeds Central continues to operate below capacity. The espresso machine on Bar 2 has now been intermittently failing for six weeks. The fault was first logged in mid-February — a pressure calibration issue that our in-house technician attempted to fix twice. Despite a parts order placed on 28 February, the replacement valve has not yet arrived. The machine is currently being managed by reducing output on busy periods, which is directly impacting throughput.

Separately, the branch received only 60% of its expected oat milk allocation in the first week of March. NorthBrew Supplies cited "logistics disruption at their Wakefield depot" as the cause. The shortfall meant we were unable to fulfil oat milk-based drinks for approximately two days. This was not communicated to customers in advance, which generated a number of in-store complaints.

**Current headcount:** 9 of 11 positions filled. Two barista roles remain vacant following the departures of Tom Okafor (resigned 14 February) and Priya Nair (transferred to Sheffield). Temporary cover arranged through agency staff, but continuity has been inconsistent.

## Manchester Piccadilly Branch

Manchester Piccadilly performed marginally better but is not without issues. The mobile ordering system, which went live on 4 March as part of the wider company rollout, experienced significant teething problems in the first fortnight. Orders were arriving with incorrect modifier data — several oat milk substitutions were not registering correctly in the POS system, leading to customer confusion and re-makes.

Two of the espresso machines at Manchester Piccadilly were flagged by the duty manager, James Rowley, as producing inconsistent extraction pressure. Engineering was contacted on 12 March and a site visit was scheduled for 19 March but subsequently rescheduled to 4 April. This delay is a concern, particularly heading into the Easter trading period.

## Supply Chain Summary

NorthBrew Supplies remains the primary concern. Oat milk deliveries have been unreliable for the past six weeks across both branches. Sarah Mitchell escalated formally to the supply chain team on 8 March. At time of writing, no resolution timeline has been confirmed.

## Actions Required

- Engineering to attend Manchester Piccadilly no later than 28 March  
- HR to fast-track Leeds Central barista recruitment  
- Supply chain to identify contingency oat milk supplier  
- Regional IT to investigate mobile ordering modifier sync issue
