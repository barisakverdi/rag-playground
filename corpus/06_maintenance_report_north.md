# Maintenance Report – North England Branches
**Period:** March 2024  
**Prepared by:** Marcus Webb, In-House Technician – North Region  
**Reviewed by:** Sarah Mitchell, Regional Operations Manager

---

## Overview

This report summarises all maintenance activity — scheduled preventative, reactive, and outstanding — for BrewPulse Coffee branches in the North England region during March 2024. Three branches are covered: Leeds Central, Manchester Piccadilly, and Sheffield Meadowhall.

---

## Leeds Central

### Reactive Maintenance – Espresso Machine Bar 2 (BP-LC-EM02)

This machine has been the primary maintenance concern in the region throughout February and March. Following the initial fault report in mid-February (logged formally as INC-2024-0312), I carried out two repair attempts:

- **20 February:** Replaced group head gaskets and recalibrated pressure profile. Machine returned to service.  
- **27 February:** Machine again reported under-pressure. Inspection confirmed valve seal failure — not addressable without replacement part.  
- **28 February:** Part ordered via BrewPulse procurement. Standard lead time quoted as 5–7 working days.

As of 12 March, the part has not arrived. I followed up with procurement on 6 March and was told the part is on back-order with the supplier. I have now sourced a compatible valve seal (non-OEM) from ProBev Engineering at a cost of £185 + VAT. Awaiting sign-off from Sarah Mitchell.

The machine is currently operational at reduced capacity. Bar 2 is being used for americanos and filter preparation where possible, with espresso-based drinks routed through Bar 1. This is not a sustainable arrangement for a location with Leeds Central's volume.

### Scheduled Maintenance – Bar 1 (BP-LC-EM01)
Routine descale and group head service completed 7 March. No faults identified. Machine in good working order.

### Grinder (BP-LC-GR01)
Burr inspection completed 14 March. Burrs showing wear but within acceptable limits. Flagged for replacement at next scheduled service (June 2024).

---

## Manchester Piccadilly

### Reactive Maintenance – Espresso Machines

Following concerns raised by duty manager James Rowley about inconsistent extraction pressure on two machines, I visited Manchester Piccadilly on **4 April** (originally scheduled 19 March but rescheduled due to a parts procurement delay — see Leeds section above, same visit was initially planned as a combined trip).

On inspection:

- **Bar 1 (BP-MP-EM01):** Pressure issue confirmed. The solenoid valve is showing early signs of failure — same class of fault as the Leeds Bar 2 machine. Temporary adjustment made; full repair requires the same valve part currently on back-order.  
- **Bar 2 (BP-MP-EM02):** No fault found on day of visit. Duty manager noted pressure issues had been intermittent. I suspect this may be linked to water hardness — Manchester's water supply sits near the upper threshold for scale accumulation, and the last descale was 5 months ago.

Descale scheduled for both Manchester Piccadilly machines within two weeks.

### Boiler Inspection
The commercial boiler serving the Manchester Piccadilly site hot water system was due its annual inspection in March. The visit was originally scheduled with ThermalPro Services for 25 March. This was postponed to 10 April due to engineer availability. This is a compliance-relevant item and should not be deferred further.
