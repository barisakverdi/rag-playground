# Supplier Update: NorthBrew Supplies – Oat Milk Allocation Disruption
**Issued by:** Supply Chain Lead – Dominic Ferrara  
**Date:** 15 March 2024  
**Affected Branches:** Leeds Central, Manchester Piccadilly, Sheffield Meadowhall  
**Supplier Contact:** Kevin Holt, Account Manager, NorthBrew Supplies

---

## Background

NorthBrew Supplies has been BrewPulse Coffee's primary oat milk supplier for the North and Midlands regions since January 2023. Under the current contract, weekly deliveries are scheduled on Tuesdays for Leeds and Manchester, and Thursdays for Sheffield. The agreed minimum fulfilment rate is 90% of the ordered volume.

BrewPulse's oat milk consumption has increased significantly since the introduction of the oat flat white as a permanent menu item in October 2023. This has made the product a higher-risk line from a supply dependency perspective.

## Summary of Disruption

Beginning in the last week of February 2024, NorthBrew Supplies experienced a logistics disruption originating at their Wakefield distribution depot. According to Kevin Holt, the depot underwent an unplanned systems migration that caused routing errors in their outbound delivery scheduling. This resulted in partial and missed deliveries to multiple accounts, not solely BrewPulse.

The direct impact on BrewPulse branches was as follows:

- **Leeds Central:** 40% shortfall in week commencing 26 February; 15% shortfall in week commencing 4 March  
- **Manchester Piccadilly:** 30% shortfall in week commencing 4 March  
- **Sheffield Meadowhall:** Minimal impact; one delayed delivery resolved within 24 hours

Sarah Mitchell formally escalated the issue to Dominic Ferrara on 8 March, noting that the Leeds shortfall had already caused a service disruption (customers turned away from oat milk-based drinks). This is documented in INC-2024-0312 and the North England Regional Ops Report for March.

## Supplier Response

Kevin Holt provided a written apology on 11 March and confirmed the systems issue has been resolved as of 9 March. NorthBrew Supplies have committed to:

1. Full allocation resumption from week commencing 18 March  
2. A credit note of £420 against March invoices to compensate for the shortfall  
3. A formal incident report from their logistics team by 22 March

At time of writing, no formal incident report from NorthBrew has been received.

## Risk Assessment

Despite the supplier's assurances, the supply chain team has concerns about NorthBrew Supplies' infrastructure. This is the second disruption from this supplier in six months — a similar (smaller) shortfall occurred in September 2023, which was attributed to a haulage partner cancellation. At that time, no formal SLA review was triggered.

Given oat milk is now a high-volume, high-dependency product, Dominic Ferrara has recommended initiating a dual-sourcing review. A secondary supplier, GreenLeaf Provisions (South Yorkshire), has been identified as a potential backup. Commercial terms are under review.

## Actions Outstanding

- NorthBrew to provide formal logistics incident report by 22 March *(overdue as of report date)*  
- Supply chain to complete dual-sourcing assessment by 5 April  
- Contract review meeting with NorthBrew to be scheduled for April  
- Branches to maintain a minimum 3-day oat milk buffer stock going forward
