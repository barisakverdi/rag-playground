# Staffing Issues Report – North Region
**Period:** January–March 2024 (Q1)  
**Prepared by:** Sarah Mitchell, Regional Operations Manager – North  
**HR Business Partner:** Gemma Holroyd  
**Distribution:** Head of People, Regional Directors

---

## Summary

The North region ends Q1 2024 with a staffing gap that is wider than at any point in the previous 12 months. A combination of voluntary departures, an internal transfer, and slower-than-expected recruitment has left two branches — Leeds Central and Sheffield Meadowhall — operating below minimum staffing thresholds for extended periods.

This report outlines the current position, contributing factors, and recommended interventions.

---

## Leeds Central

Leeds Central currently has 9 of 11 budgeted positions filled. The two vacancies arose from:

- **Tom Okafor** (Barista, Grade 2) – resigned 14 February 2024. Tom cited personal reasons but informally mentioned dissatisfaction with shift patterns during his exit conversation with the branch manager. He had been with BrewPulse for 14 months.
- **Priya Nair** (Senior Barista) – transferred to Sheffield Meadowhall effective 3 March 2024 at her request, to reduce commute distance following a house move.

Both roles were posted on 19 February (Tom's replacement) and 4 March (following Priya's transfer). As of 28 March, no suitable candidates have progressed past first interview. One candidate withdrew after receiving a higher offer from a competitor.

Agency cover has been arranged through Apex Staffing Solutions. However, continuity has been inconsistent — three different agency workers have been placed in the past month, each requiring familiarisation time. The absence of an experienced senior barista is particularly felt during morning rush periods, where the Leeds Central setup requires two skilled operators at peak times. This has compounded the impact of the espresso machine issues on Bar 2 (see INC-2024-0312).

**Immediate risk:** If no permanent hire is made before Easter (29 March), the branch will enter its highest-volume trading period significantly understaffed.

---

## Sheffield Meadowhall

Sheffield received Priya Nair on transfer but was already carrying a vacancy of its own — a barista position left open since January following the departure of Marcus Eaton. Combined with Priya's arrival bringing one new starter into a busy environment, the Sheffield branch manager (Rosa Chen) has flagged concerns about induction capacity.

Priya is experienced but requires familiarisation with the Sheffield setup and has not yet been trained on the Sheffield Meadowhall-specific inventory system. Rosa Chen has requested a two-week overlap of support from a neighbouring branch, which is not currently possible given Leeds' own constraints.

---

## Contributing Factors

Sarah Mitchell has noted a pattern over Q1 that warrants attention at a structural level:

1. **Shift pattern dissatisfaction:** Two of the three departures in the region this quarter cited shift-related reasons (either formally or informally). The current rota at Leeds Central relies heavily on split shifts, which have been unpopular with younger staff.

2. **Pay competitiveness:** The competitor offer that led to the candidate withdrawal at Leeds Central was reportedly £0.75/hour above BrewPulse's current barista Grade 2 rate. Gemma Holroyd has flagged this to the People team for a market benchmarking review.

3. **Mobile ordering rollout pressure:** The introduction of the new ordering system in early March added training and operational complexity at exactly the time when branch teams were already stretched. Several staff members informally mentioned that the rollout felt rushed.

---

## Requested Actions

- Expedite Leeds Central recruitment; consider relaxing experience requirements or introducing a structured induction programme  
- Approve temporary uplift to agency contract for April to cover Easter peak  
- People team to complete barista pay benchmarking by 15 April  
- Review shift pattern model for Leeds Central with branch manager  
- Confirm whether cross-branch support for Sheffield can be arranged from Manchester or another North branch
