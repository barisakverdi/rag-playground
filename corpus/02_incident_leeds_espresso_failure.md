# Incident Report: Espresso Machine Failure
**Branch:** Leeds Central  
**Incident Ref:** INC-2024-0312  
**Date of Initial Fault:** 15 February 2024  
**Date of This Report:** 12 March 2024  
**Reported by:** Duty Manager, Leeds Central – Daniel Park  
**Escalated to:** Sarah Mitchell, Regional Operations Manager

---

## Incident Description

The commercial espresso machine on Bar 2 (a La Marzocca Linea PB, asset tag BP-LC-EM02) has been experiencing recurring pressure failures since approximately 15 February 2024. The fault manifests as inconsistent extraction pressure, typically dropping mid-shot, resulting in under-extracted espresso. During peak service hours this causes a significant backlog as baristas switch to the Bar 1 machine.

The fault was first reported internally by senior barista Chloe Barnes and logged in the branch maintenance journal on 17 February. Initial assessment by our in-house technician, Marcus Webb, identified a likely valve seal failure. A temporary fix was applied on 20 February; however, the fault recurred within 72 hours.

| 12 Mar | This incident report escalated to regional management |

## Operational Impact

With Bar 2 effectively running at reduced capacity, Leeds Central has been operating on a single full-pressure espresso machine during peak periods. Queue times during the 8–10am window have increased by an estimated 35–40%. Two customer complaints referencing slow service were received in the first week of March and have been logged separately in the CRM system.

The issue is compounded by the current staffing shortfall at Leeds Central (see regional ops report March 2024). With two barista positions vacant, the reduced-capacity setup is being managed by fewer experienced staff than is operationally desirable.

## Contributing Factors

It is worth noting that the oat milk supply disruption experienced in the first week of March (caused by a NorthBrew Supplies depot issue) further increased pressure on bar staff during the period when the machine was already underperforming. The combination of supply and equipment issues on the same days created a particularly difficult trading environment.

## Current Status

The replacement valve has not arrived as of the date of this report. Marcus Webb has sourced a compatible part from a third-party supplier as a contingency; cost approval pending from Sarah Mitchell. If approved, installation can take place within 48 hours.

## Recommended Actions

1. Approve emergency procurement of compatible valve part (estimated cost: £185 + VAT)  
2. Review preventative maintenance schedule for all La Marzocca units across North England  
3. Consider replacement of Bar 2 unit if further faults occur post-repair  
4. Escalate to procurement: recurring delivery failures for equipment parts must be reviewed
