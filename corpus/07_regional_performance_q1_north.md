# Regional Performance Summary – North England
**Quarter:** Q1 2024 (January–March)  
**Prepared by:** Sarah Mitchell, Regional Operations Manager – North  
**Reviewed by:** Head of Operations – BrewPulse Coffee  
**Date:** 5 April 2024

---

## Overview

Q1 2024 has been a below-target quarter for the North England region. While Sheffield Meadowhall maintained consistent performance, Leeds Central and Manchester Piccadilly both underperformed against their operational KPIs. The root causes are interconnected: supplier disruption, equipment failures, staffing gaps, and the complications of a mid-quarter technology rollout have combined to create a negative feedback loop across customer satisfaction, throughput, and staff morale.

---

## Branch-by-Branch Summary

### Leeds Central

- **Transactions vs target:** -8% (March alone: -13%)  
- **Customer Satisfaction Score (CSS):** 3.1/5 (Q4 2023: 3.8/5)  
- **Avg. queue time (peak):** 6.8 minutes (target: ≤4 minutes)  
- **Staffing level:** 82% of budgeted headcount throughout March  

Leeds Central's performance deteriorated through the quarter as the espresso machine fault on Bar 2 became a persistent issue. The transaction shortfall reflects both reduced throughput capacity and a number of customers not returning after poor experiences in early March. The CSS drop from 3.8 to 3.1 is the steepest quarterly decline recorded for this branch.

The customer feedback data (compiled by Amara Osei's team) identifies espresso quality and oat milk unavailability as the two most-cited drivers of dissatisfaction. Both trace back to supply and equipment failures that were known issues for weeks before their customer-facing impact peaked.

### Manchester Piccadilly

- **Transactions vs target:** -4% (mobile ordering offset partially recovered volume)  
- **Customer Satisfaction Score (CSS):** 3.4/5 (Q4 2023: 3.7/5)  
- **Mobile ordering adoption:** 23% of transactions by end of March (target: 20% — met)  
- **Mobile ordering complaint rate:** 18% of mobile orders generated a complaint or re-make in weeks 1–2  

Manchester Piccadilly tells a more nuanced story. The mobile ordering rollout achieved its adoption target — 23% of transactions by end of March — but the complaint rate in the first two weeks was uncomfortably high, at 18% of mobile orders. The modifier sync issue (oat milk substitutions not registering correctly in the POS) drove the majority of these. This was resolved by a software patch on 18 March, and complaint rates dropped to 4% in the final two weeks.

The espresso machine pressure issues at Manchester Piccadilly, while less severe than at Leeds, contributed to a modest CSS decline. The delayed engineering visit (rescheduled from 19 March to 4 April) meant the branch entered its final trading weeks of the quarter with unresolved equipment concerns.

### Sheffield Meadowhall

- **Transactions vs target:** +2%  
- **Customer Satisfaction Score (CSS):** 4.1/5 (Q4 2023: 3.9/5)  
- **Staffing level:** 95% of budgeted headcount  

Sheffield Meadowhall was the clear outlier in Q1. Stable equipment, consistent supply, and a well-embedded team (notwithstanding the vacancy carried from January) produced solid results. The branch was also the least affected by the NorthBrew Supplies oat milk disruption. Rosa Chen's operational management has been a positive factor — she proactively increased buffer stock in February following the September 2023 NorthBrew disruption, which meant Sheffield avoided the worst of the March shortfall.

---

## Regional Trends

**Supply chain:** The NorthBrew Supplies situation remains the most significant systemic risk. Two disruptions in six months affecting high-dependency products is not acceptable. Dominic Ferrara's dual-sourcing review must be concluded before Q2.

**Equipment:** The valve failure pattern across Leeds and Manchester Piccadilly La Marzocca units is a capital planning concern. If units of this age are approaching component failure at scale, the maintenance budget may need revision.

**Technology:** Mobile ordering rollout created short-term disruption but appears to be stabilising. Manchester Piccadilly's experience will inform the rollout schedule for remaining branches.

**Staffing:** The region cannot sustain sub-90% headcount across multiple branches simultaneously. Easter trading period (from 29 March) will test whether agency cover is adequate.

---

## Q2 Outlook

Recovery is achievable but dependent on resolving the outstanding equipment faults at Leeds and Manchester, completing the Leeds Central permanent hire, and stabilising the NorthBrew supply relationship (or activating an alternative). Sarah Mitchell has requested an additional £3,500 in discretionary budget for Q2 to cover agency staffing costs above standard allocation.
