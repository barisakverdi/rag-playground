# Customer Feedback Summary
**Branches:** Leeds Central / Manchester Piccadilly  
**Period:** 1–31 March 2024  
**Compiled by:** Customer Experience Team – Amara Osei  
**Source:** In-app feedback, Google Reviews, in-store complaint cards

---

## Overview

A total of 47 customer feedback submissions were received across the two branches in March 2024 — a 38% increase on February (34 submissions). Of these, 29 were classified as negative or mixed, representing a dissatisfaction rate of 61.7%. This is significantly above the company benchmark of 25%.

Both branches show similar complaint themes, suggesting systemic rather than branch-specific issues are driving negative sentiment.

---

## Leeds Central – Key Themes

**Volume received:** 26 submissions (18 negative, 6 mixed, 2 positive)

### 1. Drink Quality / Espresso Issues
Eleven customers specifically referenced weak or "watery" espresso. Language used includes phrases such as "flat taste," "not strong enough," and "not like usual." These complaints are concentrated in the 8–10am peak window and correlate directly with the ongoing Bar 2 machine failure documented in INC-2024-0312. Customers appear to be detecting the under-extraction issue even without awareness of the underlying equipment fault.

### 2. Oat Milk Unavailability
Six customers complained about being told oat milk was unavailable. Three of these referenced specific dates in the first week of March — consistent with the NorthBrew Supplies delivery shortfall. Two customers noted they were not warned in advance via the app or in-store signage, and one submitted a formal written complaint requesting a refund for a journey made specifically for an oat flat white.

### 3. Wait Times
Eight complaints cited excessive queuing, particularly during the morning rush. Several customers mentioned being told the "coffee machine was playing up." This is a transparency concern: while staff were trying to be helpful, informal references to equipment failures risk undermining customer confidence.

---

## Manchester Piccadilly – Key Themes

**Volume received:** 21 submissions (11 negative, 6 mixed, 4 positive)

### 1. Mobile Ordering Problems
Nine customers submitted feedback specifically about the mobile ordering experience. The most common issues: orders not ready on arrival, incorrect milk alternatives (particularly oat milk not applied despite being selected in-app), and one case of a duplicate charge. These issues map directly to the modifier sync problem identified in the March regional ops report — the POS integration bug that affected oat milk substitutions.

### 2. Staff Confusion / Communication
Four customers referenced staff appearing uncertain about mobile order procedures. One Google review described staff as "clearly undertrained on the new system." This is understandable given the app launched only four weeks before month-end, but it suggests the rollout support and training provision was insufficient.

### 3. Drink Quality
Four complaints about espresso quality, with similar language to Leeds ("bland," "weak"). Two of these were submitted on 18 and 19 March — the days following the rescheduled (and then delayed) engineering visit for the pressure issues on Manchester Piccadilly's machines.

---

## Cross-Branch Observations

The coincidence of oat milk shortages, espresso machine issues, and the mobile ordering rollout across the same period has amplified customer dissatisfaction beyond what any single issue would have caused in isolation. Customers experiencing a mobile ordering glitch alongside unavailable oat milk and slow service are significantly more likely to leave negative reviews.

Amara Osei recommends a coordinated response plan, including a proactive customer email for verified app users at both branches and a temporary service guarantee offer.
